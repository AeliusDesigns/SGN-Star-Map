Foreword
This document is prepared by the Office of Foreign Affairs and Diplomatic Engagement of Arandorë Eldainë and issued to foreign diplomatic entities, prospective state partners, and any party seeking an informed understanding of the High Realm and its people. It is intended as a comprehensive introduction: an account of what Arandorë Eldainë is, how it is governed, what it values, and what it expects of those who would engage with it in good faith.
The Realm does not consider it necessary to obscure what it is in order to be found agreeable. Foreign partners who approach Arandorë Eldainë with accurate expectations will find the relationship more productive than those who arrive with assumptions formed in the absence of information. This document is offered in that spirit. It is complete, it is honest, and it represents the considered position of the Office on how the Realm wishes to be understood by the wider galaxy.
I
History & Founding
Arandorë Eldainë is among the oldest continuous political entities in the known galaxy. Its institutional roots reach back to the Aevum Concordiäte, the age of unification in which the Seven Great Houses formally constituted the Valarindë as their shared governing council, established the institutions that persist in evolved form to the present day, and founded the Elenar Credit as the currency of a unified Realm. The civilization that preceded this unification is older still, traced by the Realm's own scholars to the Aevum Primordialis, the first age of elven history, when the gods of the Elyndarûm walked openly among their creations.
The ages that followed were not without catastrophe. The Aevum Belli, the age of war, brought the kinslaying of Thalrion Mirithor, the discovery and misuse of the Axiom Sphere, the release of the Zahkaran beneath the dwarven range, and the collapse of every great empire of that era. Arandorë Eldainë survived what others did not. The Valarindë held. The institutions the High Elves had built proved more durable than the pressures brought to bear against them.
Foreign partners are encouraged to understand that this history is not ceremonial context. It is the active foundation of how the Realm thinks about itself, its obligations, and its relationships. A civilization that has survived what Arandorë Eldainë has survived does not treat stability as an abstract good. It treats it as the product of sustained and deliberate effort.
II
Territory & Holdings
The territorial space of Arandorë Eldainë spans multiple systems across the Twin Galaxies. The capital and administrative center is the Anorvalas Ecumenopolis, the great world-city whose urban coverage extends across the majority of the planet's habitable surface, housing the Realm's core governmental, military, financial, and diplomatic institutions.

Territorial Boundaries
The Realm's outer boundary, including the Outer Rim and Mid Rim corridor, is actively patrolled and defended following the Korban invasion. The precise extent of claimed territorial space is available to registered diplomatic partners through the Office of Foreign Affairs upon formal request.
III
Government & Structure
Arandorë Eldainë is governed by the Valarindë, the Council of the Seven Great Houses, which functions simultaneously as the Realm's legislative body, its highest court, and the primary executive authority. Presiding over the Valarindë is the High King, a position currently held by Aelorion, whose authority serves as the embodiment of elven continuity and unity.
Lineage Governance
The Wood Elves govern through decentralized councils of circles with meaningful autonomy, answerable to an appointed High Elf steward. The Drow operate through councils of Matriarchs whose jurisdiction is defined and bounded by the Valarindë through a liaison structure. Both reflect the Realm's approach: unified at fundamental law and foreign policy, with administrative diversity within those bounds.
Diplomatic Routing
The Realm does not operate through consensus with external parties on matters it considers internal to its sovereign jurisdiction. Foreign partners should direct representations to the Office of Foreign Affairs, which will route them to the appropriate body. Direct approaches to individual houses or subsidiary governance bodies are not the preferred channel.
IV
The Four Lineages
The population comprises four distinct elven lineages. Foreign partners are encouraged to understand these distinctions, as the four lineages are meaningfully different in their cultures, values, and ways of engaging with the wider world. Treating them as interchangeable will produce confusion in diplomatic contexts.
Ξ
Eldest Lineage · Governing
Taralindë — High Elves
The eldest and most prominent elven lineage, the High Elves are the architects of Arandorë Eldainë's foundational institutions and the lineage from which the Valarindë draws its authority. Tall, luminous, and long-lived to approximately 2,500 years, they are defined above all else by their reverence for knowledge and their sense of civilizational continuity. They built the Realm, maintain it, and consider themselves its stewards by right and by burden. Their cities are grand, their archives ancient, and their memory long. They do not forget what it cost to build what they have.
~2,500 Year Lifespan Tallest Lineage Institutional Founders Knowledge Tradition Tech & Precision Industry
❀
Second Lineage · Allied
Sylvarindë — Wood Elves
A peaceable offshoot of the druidic tradition of House Sylvaris, the Wood Elves departed from the great houses during the reconstruction period to follow more fully the teachings of Shilvaon, the Singer of Nature. Shorter and more lightly built than their High Elf kin, they are defined by their relationship with the living world: as cultivators, stewards, and ecological engineers of remarkable capability. Their governance is decentralized and consensus-based, their culture rooted in seasonal cycles rather than institutional memory. They remain close to the High Elves in spirit and in politics, the most straightforward relationship between any two lineages in the Realm.
~1,500 Year Lifespan Druidic Tradition Ecological Engineers Agricultural Producers Consensus Governance
◓
Third Lineage · Restricted Standing
Umbrarindë — Drow
The Drow were not always what they are. They descend from the followers of Thalrion Mirithor, whose kinslaying in the Aevum Belli drew Farnunsa's judgment upon them: a transformation that reflected outwardly what they had already become within. They fled underground, adapted to darkness across millennia, and built a civilization shaped entirely by the pressure of survival. Shorter, darker, and biologically immortal though rarely long-lived in practice, the Drow are defined by pragmatism, meritocracy, and a distrust of everything that cannot prove its own reliability. Within the Realm they occupy the lowest civic tier, their autonomy narrow and their standing deliberately ambiguous. They are necessary, capable, and almost universally regarded with suspicion. They are aware of all three.
~750 Year Lifespan Meritocratic Subterranean Origin Industrial Fabrication Lowest Civic Tier
☼
Fourth Lineage · Allied
Varantharindë — Sand Elves
The Varantharindë are the desert-born lineage of elvenkind, shaped across millennia by the extreme conditions of Varanthas, a world orbiting the binary Calvareth system where surface temperatures swing from 35°F at true night to 340°F at peak second burning. They are a people defined by endurance, spiritual depth, and an intimate understanding of the land that sustains them. Their civilization is built into the canyon systems of Varanthas, where they share their world with the Varath, the great filter-feeding sandworms they revere as divine changers of the land. Unified once under Varathar Kael, the only elf to ride a grandfather Varath, their political and spiritual leadership has remained divided since his death.
~550 Year Lifespan Desert Adapted Clan Governance Spiritual Tradition Varath Reverence
V
Military & Defense
The armed forces consist of the Arandorë Eldainë Navy (AEN), responsible for interstellar operations, and the Arandorë Eldainë Marine Corps (AEMC), responsible for ground and boarding operations. Both services have undergone significant expansion following the Korban invasion. The Navy has more than tripled in vessel count and marine complement since that period.
The Realm's military posture is defensive in orientation but not passive in capability. Arandorë Eldainë does not seek conflict, has not historically initiated military action without provocation, and does not regard expansion as an institutional objective. What it does regard as non-negotiable is the integrity of its sovereign space. Unauthorized entry is treated as a hostile act. The response is proportionate, swift, and does not require a second provocation to be decisive.
The Shield World program, centerd on Sylcalen Araneth and the Foundry Kalyndor Nolvarin, represents the Realm's investment in long-term civilizational continuity beyond conventional military defense. Six Shield Worlds have been completed. Foreign partners are invited to note this not as a statement of pessimism but as a statement of seriousness.
ⓘ
Defense Alliances
The Realm currently maintains active defensive alliance agreements with the Golden Cohort and the Union of Lavinian Systems. These bilateral defense arrangements were entered into on terms acceptable to the Valarindë and remain in effect. Parties seeking defense cooperation are advised to engage through the Office of Foreign Affairs. The Realm does not enter arrangements that compromise its command authority or freedom of action within its own territorial space.
VI
Economy & Trade
The economy is anchored in high technology production, advanced R&D, and agricultural output at interstellar scale. Each lineage contributes distinctly: the High Elves produce advanced technology, propulsion, weapons, and precision components; the Wood Elves are the Realm's primary agricultural producers at industrial scale, including the Orwood space habitat program; the Drow contribute industrial fabrication, hull production, military hardware, and excavation capabilities.
The Elenar Credit
The Realm's sovereign currency, denominated in physical gold, silver, and copper tablets whose value is sustained by active industrial demand rather than arbitrary reserve convention. The Credit has maintained its value across every age of the Realm's existence and is accepted by most entities that trade regularly with Arandorë Eldainë.
Trade Access
Conducted through licensed channels registered with the Office of Foreign Affairs. The Realm maintains export controls on sensitive High Elf precision technology. Agricultural products, fabricated hulls, and industrial components are more freely available. The Realm is a significant purchaser of raw materials, particularly processed metals and micronutrient compounds.
VII
Culture & Values
The deepest value of Arandorë Eldainë is continuity. The Realm exists across a timescale that most civilizations do not reach and cannot fully conceptualize, and everything about how it is structured reflects the understanding of a people who have learned what it costs to lose things and have organized their entire civilization around not losing them again.
Knowledge is the currency the Realm values above all others. The great archives and crystal libraries, maintained since the Aevum Concordiäte, represent an accumulation of recorded experience with no equivalent in most of the galaxy. The Wood Elves carry an equally deep knowledge in a different register: living tradition and practical mastery of natural systems. Even the Drow have produced a body of technical knowledge in their areas of specialization that the Realm recognizes as genuinely valuable.
The Elyndarûm
The Realm has no state religion in the formal sense, though the traditions of the Elyndarûm, the elven divine pantheon, are deeply woven into the cultural life of all three lineages at different depths and in different forms. The Loremasters Guild maintains the theological and historical records. Foreign partners are not expected to share these traditions but are expected to treat them with the respect owed to things that are genuinely central to the identity of the people they are dealing with.
VIII
Diplomatic Expectations
Arandorë Eldainë recognizes the sovereignty of other states and expects the same in return. The Realm's borders, laws, internal governance, and population policies are not subject to negotiation with external parties. Representations on these matters will be received, noted, and filed. They will not produce the outcomes the representing party may be seeking.
The Realm is a patient negotiator and a reliable partner to those who deal with it honestly. Its institutional memory is long and its record-keeping is precise: commitments made to Arandorë Eldainë are remembered in their exact terms, and the Realm holds the same standard for itself.
The Realm does not respond well to pressure tactics, ultimatums, or the framing of requests as demands. A civilization that has endured what Arandorë Eldainë has endured is not easily moved by the implication that it has more to lose from a disagreement than the party making the implication.
✉
Establishing Diplomatic Relations
Parties seeking formal relations should direct initial contact to the Office of Foreign Affairs through registered diplomatic channels. The Office will acknowledge receipt, conduct preliminary review, and arrange engagement at the appropriate level. The Realm welcomes genuine partners. It is not difficult to become one, provided the approach is made in good faith and with accurate expectations.
